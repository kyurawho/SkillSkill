FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about working at VisualSkill.

Our hiring process typically consists of four stages: (1) an initial screening call with HR (30 mins), (2) a technical assessment relevant to the role, (3) a technical interview with the team (60 mins), and (4) a final culture fit interview with a team lead. We aim to complete the entire process within 2–3 weeks. We respect your time and provide feedback at every stage.

Yes! Many of our positions are fully remote or hybrid. We believe the best talent can be found anywhere in the world, and we've built our culture and tools around async-first collaboration. Specific remote policies vary by role — check each job listing for details. We also have offices in Jakarta, Bandung, and Surabaya for those who prefer in-person work.

We offer a comprehensive benefits package including: competitive salary, annual performance bonuses, flexible working hours, health insurance (medical, dental, vision), 20 days paid time off + public holidays, free access to all VisualSkill courses, a learning & development budget of $1,000/year, home office stipend for remote employees, and team retreats twice a year. We genuinely invest in our people's growth.

Absolutely! Job descriptions list ideal qualifications, but we know that the best candidates don't always check every box. What matters most to us is your potential, eagerness to learn, and cultural fit. If you're excited about the role and believe you can contribute meaningfully, we encourage you to apply and let us have a conversation. We've hired many people who didn't meet every listed requirement.

Growth is at the heart of what we do. Every employee gets free unlimited access to all VisualSkill courses, a dedicated $1,000 annual learning budget for external courses, books, or conferences, a personal learning plan developed with your manager, regular 1-on-1 coaching sessions, internal knowledge-sharing talks, and a clear career progression framework. We believe that a company focused on education should lead by example.
